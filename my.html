Renault Logan models

The world's best-selling French car brand, Renault has been meeting its customers' mobility needs for over 125 years. A pioneer in the creation of "voitures à vivre," the brand designs popular avant-garde vehicles, making cutting-edge technologies accessible to everyone. As part of its commitment to reducing its carbon footprint, Renault has developed a two-pronged range of electric and hybrid vehicles allowing everyone to move towards the energy transition at their own pace.Renault designs models that are in tune with customer needs and the challenges of today. For instance, the Ingenius rear armrest in Scénic E-Tech Electric and Rafale creates a true relaxation space in the back, featuring a screen holder, cup holders, and USB-C ports. The sliding rear bench in Captur and Austral redefines interior space, while the 7-seat configurations in Kangoo and Espace offer uncompromising flexibility. At Renault, technology is designed to be human and accessible, while ensuring top-level safety through innovative systems that facilitate emergency response in the event of an accident.

Renault megane models

What is it? Renault’s Megane dates back all the way to 1995, and the version currently on sale is the fourth generation of the French company’s stalwart family hatchback. You’d think that it would be an absolutely bread and butter model, but with people moving to fancy electric cars or more fashionable SUVs, the humble Megane has been somewhat left behind. Didn't this used to be stylish? It did use to look a lot cooler, perhaps that’s part of the issue. The second generation was quite the looker, especially when it started shaking its backside for the adverts (you might have to look that one up), but now the car has drifted off so far into anonymity you’d have trouble finding it in a car park again. The front end with its conjunctivitis-style DRL was distinctive when it was first pasted on the Megane, but now it has been replicated across the whole Renault range it’s lost some of the charm.
